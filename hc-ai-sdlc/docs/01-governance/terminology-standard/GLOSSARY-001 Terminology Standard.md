# GLOSSARY-001 — Terminology Standard

> **Normative Governance Standard**
>
> This standard establishes the official terminology used throughout the Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence Software Development Lifecycle (HC-AI SDLC).
>
> Every authoritative document within the framework shall use these terms consistently. Where ambiguity exists, the definitions contained within this standard shall prevail.

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this standard is to establish a single, authoritative vocabulary for the HC-AI SDLC framework.

Consistent terminology enables clear communication, reduces ambiguity, improves traceability, and ensures that humans and AI assistants interpret governance, operational, and engineering concepts consistently.

This standard serves as the canonical source for terminology throughout the framework.

---

# 2. Terminology Principles

The terminology defined within this standard shall follow these principles:

- Every term has one authoritative definition.
- A term shall not have multiple meanings within the framework.
- Definitions shall remain technology-neutral whenever possible.
- Definitions shall describe concepts rather than implementation details.
- New terminology shall be introduced only when necessary.

---

# 3. Core Terminology

## Artifact

A tangible output produced during the execution of a lifecycle.

Examples include documents, source code, specifications, templates, diagrams, reports, models, and configuration files.

Every artifact shall belong to a clearly defined domain and lifecycle.

---

## Capability

A distinct business, organizational, or engineering ability that enables desired outcomes.

Capabilities describe **what** must be achieved rather than **how** it is implemented.

---

## Charter

A constitutional governance document that establishes the foundational purpose, principles, commitments, and governance scope of a framework or domain.

A Charter operationalizes constitutional authority without replacing or redefining the Constitution.

---

## Domain

A bounded area of responsibility within the HC-AI SDLC.

Each domain owns a distinct set of responsibilities, authority, and artifacts.

Examples include Governance, Operations, Systems, Engineering Operating System (EOS), Products, Records, and Legacy.

---

## Engineering Operating System (EOS)

The implementation environment that provides reusable engineering assets required to execute the Operational Model.

Examples include templates, blueprints, starter kits, tooling, automation, reusable assets, and engineering workflows.

---

## Governance

The system of authority, principles, standards, and responsibilities that directs and constrains the operation and evolution of the HC-AI SDLC.

Governance establishes **what must be followed** throughout the framework.

---

## Operational Model

The abstract representation of how work is performed within the HC-AI SDLC.

It defines the structure, flow, responsibilities, and interactions required to execute governed engineering activities independently of specific technologies or tools.

---

## Product

A software system, platform, service, or solution developed under the governance of the HC-AI SDLC framework.

Products inherit all applicable governance, operational, and engineering standards.

---

## Record

An artifact that documents evidence of work performed, decisions made, observations recorded, or verification completed.

Records describe execution and outcomes.

Records possess no governing authority.

---

## Standard

A normative document that establishes mandatory rules, requirements, constraints, or expectations within a defined scope.

Standards govern implementation and promote consistency throughout the framework.

---

## State

The current verifiable condition of a system, artifact, process, or lifecycle at a specific point in time.

States enable traceability, monitoring, and verification.

---

## System

A coordinated set of rules, processes, standards, and responsibilities that govern a particular capability or operational area.

Systems translate governance into structured implementation.

---

## Traceability

The ability to identify and verify relationships between governance, requirements, decisions, artifacts, implementation, verification, and outcomes throughout the lifecycle.

Traceability supports transparency, accountability, and continuous improvement.

---

# 4. Terminology Governance

Every authoritative document within the HC-AI SDLC framework shall use terminology consistently with this standard.

Where a term is defined within GLOSSARY-001, alternative definitions shall not be introduced by subordinate documents.

If a new term is required, it shall be formally added to this standard before becoming part of the framework vocabulary.

---

# 5. Evolution

This standard may evolve as the HC-AI SDLC framework expands.

However, changes shall:

- Preserve conceptual consistency.
- Avoid redefining existing terms without justification.
- Minimize unnecessary terminology growth.
- Maintain backward compatibility whenever reasonably possible.

---

# 6. Compliance

Every authoritative document shall comply with this standard by:

- Using defined terminology consistently.
- Avoiding conflicting definitions.
- Referencing canonical terminology where appropriate.
- Maintaining semantic consistency throughout the framework.

Compliance shall be verified during governance review and document verification.

---

# Terminology Declaration

The Terminology Standard establishes the canonical vocabulary of the HC-AI SDLC framework.

Its purpose is to ensure that humans and AI assistants interpret governance, operational, and engineering concepts consistently across every authoritative document.

This standard possesses no independent constitutional authority.

Its governing authority derives entirely from **GHS-001**, which derives its authority from **CHARTER-001**, which derives its constitutional legitimacy from the **Universal Constitution for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence (HC-001)**.

Accordingly, this standard shall always be interpreted, implemented, and maintained in complete alignment with every higher authority within the constitutional governance hierarchy.
