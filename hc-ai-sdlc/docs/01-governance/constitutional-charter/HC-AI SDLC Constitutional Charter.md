# HC-AI SDLC Constitutional Charter

> **Normative Governance Document**
>
> This Constitutional Charter establishes how the **Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence Software Development Lifecycle (HC-AI SDLC)** operationalizes the principles established by the **Universal Constitution for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence (HC-001)**.
>
> This Charter **does not replace, reinterpret, summarize, amend, or compete with** HC-001. Its sole purpose is to establish the constitutional governance of the HC-AI SDLC framework and define how constitutional principles are inherited throughout the framework.

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Charter is to establish the constitutional foundation of the Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence Software Development Lifecycle (HC-AI SDLC).

This Charter defines how every governance document, operational standard, engineering standard, verification standard, specification standard, process, template, blueprint, Engineering Operating System (EOS), and product within the framework shall derive **their governing authority** from the Universal Constitution for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence (HC-001).

This Charter serves as the constitutional bridge between enduring human-centered principles and their practical implementation throughout the software development lifecycle.

---

# 2. Constitutional Authority

The Universal Constitution for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence (HC-001) is the **supreme constitutional authority** of the HC-AI SDLC framework.

All authoritative documents within the HC-AI SDLC framework derive their constitutional legitimacy through HC-001.

Where any subordinate document conflicts with HC-001, the Constitution shall prevail.

No document may supersede, contradict, weaken, reinterpret, or replace any constitutional principle established within HC-001.

---

# 3. Constitutional Preservation

The constitutional text contained within HC-001 shall remain the single canonical source of constitutional principles.

Subordinate documents shall inherit constitutional principles by reference rather than by duplication.

No document within the HC-AI SDLC framework shall:

- Rewrite constitutional articles.
- Replace constitutional language with alternative wording.
- Introduce competing constitutional principles.
- Modify constitutional intent through operational language.
- Present itself as an independent constitutional authority.

Operational guidance shall always remain subordinate to constitutional authority.

---

# 4. Scope

This Charter governs every authoritative document within the HC-AI SDLC framework, including but not limited to:

- Governance documents
- Operational standards
- Engineering standards
- Verification standards
- Specification standards
- Processes
- Profiles
- Templates
- Blueprints
- Starter Kits
- Engineering Operating System (EOS)
- Repository governance
- Products developed under the framework

Documents outside the HC-AI SDLC framework are not governed by this Charter unless they voluntarily adopt it.

---

No document may bypass, circumvent, or establish an alternative governance path.

---

# 6. Constitutional Inheritance

Every authoritative document shall explicitly declare:

- Its governing authority.
- Its inheritance relationship.
- Its responsibility within the framework.

Inheritance transfers **governing authority and applicable obligations**.

Inheritance shall not duplicate constitutional content.

Each document shall contribute responsibilities unique to its scope while remaining fully consistent with every higher authority.

---

# 7. Separation of Responsibilities

The HC-AI SDLC framework follows the principle of single responsibility across governance.

Each authoritative document shall own one primary governance responsibility.

Documents shall complement one another without overlapping authority.

Where responsibilities intersect, governance precedence shall always follow the established constitutional hierarchy.

---

# 8. Constitutional Compliance

Every authoritative document shall demonstrate alignment with the constitutional principles established by HC-001.

This includes, but is not limited to:

- Human dignity
- Human agency
- Human responsibility
- Transparency
- Truthfulness
- Accountability
- Privacy
- Fairness
- Responsible innovation
- Continuous learning

Constitutional compliance shall be demonstrated through implementation rather than declaration alone.

---

# 9. Constitutional Interpretation

Where uncertainty exists regarding the interpretation of this Charter, interpretation shall always favor the intent and principles established by HC-001.

No interpretation of this Charter shall weaken, circumvent, contradict, or diminish the constitutional protections established by the Universal Constitution for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence.

Where ambiguity remains, HC-001 shall be regarded as the sole authoritative source of constitutional interpretation.

---

# 10. Constitutional Amendments

This Charter may evolve as the HC-AI SDLC framework matures.

However, no amendment shall:

- Reduce constitutional protections.
- Override HC-001.
- Establish competing constitutional principles.
- Weaken human-centered governance.
- Create constitutional obligations inconsistent with HC-001.

Every amendment shall strengthen governance while preserving complete constitutional continuity.

---

# 11. Constitutional Integrity

HC-001 shall remain the single canonical constitutional document of the HC-AI SDLC framework.

This Charter shall never be interpreted as granting permission to modify, restate, summarize, supersede, or replace any constitutional provision contained within HC-001.

Future revisions to this Charter shall preserve full constitutional compatibility with HC-001.

---

# 12. Constitutional Commitment

By voluntarily adopting the HC-AI SDLC framework, individuals, teams, and organizations commit to implementing software engineering practices that remain fully consistent with the Universal Constitution for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence.

The framework exists to operationalize constitutional principles through responsible engineering, transparent governance, continuous learning, and accountable human oversight.

Every authoritative document within the HC-AI SDLC framework shall therefore serve the Constitution rather than redefine it.

---

# Constitutional Declaration

This Charter serves as the constitutional bridge between the enduring principles established by HC-001 and the governance of the Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence Software Development Lifecycle.

Its purpose is to ensure that constitutional values remain intact as they are translated into governance standards, operational models, engineering systems, processes, templates, and products.

**This Charter possesses no independent constitutional authority. Its authority derives entirely from HC-001.**

Accordingly, this Charter shall always be interpreted, implemented, maintained, and evolved in complete constitutional alignment with the Universal Constitution for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence.
