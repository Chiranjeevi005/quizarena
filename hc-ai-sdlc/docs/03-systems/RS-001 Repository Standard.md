# RS-001 — Repository Standard

> **Normative System Standard**
>
> This standard establishes the principles, structure, organization, and management requirements for repositories maintained within the Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence Software Development Lifecycle (HC-AI SDLC).
>
> Repositories provide the authoritative home for governance documents, operational assets, engineering artifacts, reusable assets, products, and historical records.
>
> This standard derives its governing authority from **OM-001** and ultimately from the **Universal Constitution for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence (HC-001)**.

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this standard is to establish a consistent repository architecture that supports discoverability, maintainability, traceability, governance, and long-term evolution.

A repository shall serve as the single source of truth for authoritative engineering assets.

---

# 2. Objectives

The Repository Standard exists to ensure repositories are:

- Well organized
- Consistent
- Traceable
- Governed
- Maintainable
- Scalable
- Secure
- Reusable

---

# 3. Repository Principles

Repository management within the HC-AI SDLC shall follow these principles.

## 3.1 Single Source of Truth

Each authoritative artifact shall have one canonical location.

Duplicate authoritative content shall be avoided.

---

## 3.2 Structured Organization

Repository contents shall follow a consistent information architecture.

Artifacts shall be organized according to their responsibility rather than convenience.

---

## 3.3 Consistency

Repository naming, metadata, directory structures, and document organization shall remain consistent throughout the framework.

---

## 3.4 Traceability

Repository contents shall preserve traceability between:

- Governance
- Operational standards
- Engineering artifacts
- Specifications
- Verification evidence
- Products
- Records

---

## 3.5 Version Control

Repository history shall preserve meaningful changes while supporting collaboration and auditability.

---

## 3.6 Reusability

Reusable assets shall be maintained separately from project-specific implementation whenever appropriate.

---

## 3.7 Scalability

Repository organization shall support long-term growth without requiring structural redesign.

---

# 4. Repository Structure

Repositories shall organize information into clearly defined domains.

Typical domains include:

- Governance
- Operational
- Systems
- Engineering Operating System
- Products
- Records
- Legacy

Each domain shall have a clearly defined responsibility.

---

# 5. Repository Artifacts

Repositories may contain:

- Standards
- Specifications
- Source code
- Templates
- Blueprints
- Guides
- Documentation
- Configuration
- Research
- Verification evidence
- Records

Artifacts shall remain version-controlled and traceable.

---

# 6. Repository Organization

Repository organization shall promote:

- Discoverability
- Consistency
- Separation of responsibilities
- Reuse
- Long-term maintenance

Directory structures shall reflect architectural responsibilities rather than individual projects.

---

# 7. Repository Metadata

Authoritative repository artifacts shall maintain standardized metadata where applicable.

Metadata shall support:

- Identification
- Governance
- Versioning
- Ownership
- Lifecycle management
- Traceability

---

# 8. Repository Maintenance

Repositories shall be actively maintained.

Maintenance activities include:

- Refactoring
- Organization
- Archiving
- Dependency management
- Documentation updates
- Structure improvements

Maintenance shall preserve governance alignment and repository integrity.

---

# 9. Repository Quality

Repository quality shall emphasize:

- Organization
- Readability
- Consistency
- Accuracy
- Maintainability
- Traceability
- Accessibility

Quality shall be continuously monitored and improved.

---

# 10. Repository Evolution

Repositories may evolve as engineering practices, technologies, and organizational needs change.

Evolution shall:

- Preserve architectural integrity.
- Maintain traceability.
- Avoid unnecessary restructuring.
- Preserve backward compatibility where appropriate.

---

# 11. Compliance

Repositories shall comply with this standard by:

- Following approved organizational structures.
- Maintaining traceable artifacts.
- Preserving repository integrity.
- Supporting governance.
- Supporting operational execution.
- Maintaining consistent repository practices.

Compliance shall be verified through repository review and verification activities.

---

# Repository Declaration

The Repository Standard establishes the requirements for organizing and maintaining repositories throughout the HC-AI SDLC framework.

Its purpose is to ensure that repositories remain structured, traceable, maintainable, and aligned with governance, operational, and engineering standards throughout their lifecycle.

This standard possesses no independent constitutional authority.

Its governing authority derives entirely from **OM-001**, which derives its authority from **AOS-001**, which derives its authority from **IAS-001**, which derives its authority from **GHS-001**, which derives its authority from **CHARTER-001**, which derives its constitutional legitimacy from the **Universal Constitution for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence (HC-001)**.

Accordingly, this standard shall always be interpreted, implemented, and maintained in complete alignment with every higher authority within the constitutional governance hierarchy.
