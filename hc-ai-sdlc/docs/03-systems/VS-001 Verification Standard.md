# VS-001 — Verification Standard

> **Normative System Standard**
>
> This standard establishes the verification principles, responsibilities, activities, and quality expectations for all verification performed within the Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence Software Development Lifecycle (HC-AI SDLC).
>
> Verification ensures that engineering artifacts satisfy defined requirements, comply with applicable standards, and maintain quality throughout the engineering lifecycle.
>
> This standard derives its governing authority from **OM-001** and ultimately from the **Universal Constitution for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence (HC-001)**.

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this standard is to establish a consistent verification framework throughout the HC-AI SDLC.

Verification shall provide objective evidence that engineering activities and artifacts conform to applicable governance, operational, engineering, and product requirements.

---

# 2. Objectives

The Verification Standard exists to ensure that verification activities are:

- Continuous
- Objective
- Traceable
- Repeatable
- Evidence-based
- Risk-aware
- Governed
- Consistent

---

# 3. Verification Principles

Verification within the HC-AI SDLC shall follow these principles.

## 3.1 Continuous Verification

Verification shall occur throughout the lifecycle rather than only after implementation.

---

## 3.2 Independence

Verification shall remain objective.

Where appropriate, verification should be performed by individuals or systems independent of the original implementation.

---

## 3.3 Evidence-Based

Verification conclusions shall be supported by objective evidence.

Opinions alone shall not constitute verification.

---

## 3.4 Traceability

Every verification activity shall be traceable to:

- Requirements
- Standards
- Specifications
- Engineering artifacts
- Decisions
- Records

---

## 3.5 Repeatability

Verification activities shall be sufficiently documented to enable consistent repetition under comparable conditions.

---

## 3.6 Transparency

Verification findings shall clearly distinguish between:

- Verified compliance
- Observations
- Risks
- Recommendations
- Non-conformities

---

## 3.7 Continuous Improvement

Verification shall contribute to improving engineering quality, operational effectiveness, and framework maturity.

---

# 4. Verification Lifecycle

Verification activities shall be integrated throughout the engineering lifecycle.

Typical verification stages include:

- Planning
- Review
- Inspection
- Assessment
- Validation Support
- Approval
- Continuous Monitoring

Verification shall support every operational stage defined by the Operational Model.

---

# 5. Verification Activities

Verification activities may include:

- Requirements review
- Architecture review
- Design review
- Code review
- Documentation review
- Security review
- Testing verification
- Compliance assessment
- Release verification
- Operational verification

Activities shall remain technology-independent.

---

# 6. Verification Evidence

Verification shall produce objective evidence.

Examples include:

- Review reports
- Checklists
- Test results
- Audit records
- Approval records
- Compliance reports
- Validation reports
- Issue logs

Verification evidence shall be retained according to applicable repository and record management standards.

---

# 7. Quality Gates

Verification shall establish quality gates throughout the lifecycle.

Quality gates determine whether work is ready to progress to the next operational stage.

Quality gates shall be:

- Clearly defined
- Measurable
- Repeatable
- Traceable
- Governed

---

# 8. Verification Responsibilities

Verification responsibilities include:

- Planning verification activities
- Performing reviews
- Evaluating compliance
- Recording evidence
- Identifying risks
- Reporting findings
- Supporting corrective actions
- Confirming resolution

Verification supports engineering quality but does not replace engineering responsibility.

---

# 9. Verification Outcomes

Verification activities may result in:

- Approved
- Approved with observations
- Rework required
- Non-compliant
- Deferred
- Rejected

Outcome definitions shall be consistently applied across the framework.

---

# 10. Verification Traceability

Verification shall maintain traceability between:

- Requirements
- Standards
- Engineering artifacts
- Verification activities
- Evidence
- Findings
- Decisions
- Releases

Traceability enables accountability, auditing, and continuous improvement.

---

# 11. Verification Compliance

Verification activities shall confirm compliance with applicable:

- Governance Standards
- Operational Standards
- Engineering Standards
- Repository Standards
- Product Standards
- Organizational policies

Compliance verification shall be documented.

---

# 12. Verification Evolution

Verification practices may evolve as engineering methods and technologies advance.

Evolution shall:

- Preserve governance alignment.
- Improve verification effectiveness.
- Increase automation where appropriate.
- Maintain consistency and repeatability.

---

# 13. Compliance

Verification activities shall comply with this standard by:

- Following defined verification processes.
- Producing objective evidence.
- Maintaining traceability.
- Applying quality gates consistently.
- Recording verification outcomes.
- Supporting continuous improvement.

Compliance shall be verified through governance review and operational assessment.

---

# Verification Declaration

The Verification Standard establishes the verification expectations for all engineering work performed within the HC-AI SDLC framework.

Its purpose is to ensure that engineering artifacts, processes, and products are objectively evaluated for quality, compliance, completeness, and fitness before progression through the lifecycle.

This standard possesses no independent constitutional authority.

Its governing authority derives entirely from **OM-001**, which derives its authority from **AOS-001**, which derives its authority from **IAS-001**, which derives its authority from **GHS-001**, which derives its authority from **CHARTER-001**, which derives its constitutional legitimacy from the **Universal Constitution for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence (HC-001)**.

Accordingly, this standard shall always be interpreted, implemented, and maintained in complete alignment with every higher authority within the constitutional governance hierarchy.
