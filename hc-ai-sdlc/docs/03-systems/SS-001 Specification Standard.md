# SS-001 — Specification Standard

> **Normative System Standard**
>
> This standard establishes the principles, structure, and quality expectations for specifications produced within the Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence Software Development Lifecycle (HC-AI SDLC).
>
> Specifications define what shall be built, why it is required, and the expected outcomes before implementation begins.
>
> This standard derives its governing authority from **OM-001** and ultimately from the **Universal Constitution for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence (HC-001)**.

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this standard is to establish a consistent approach for creating, maintaining, reviewing, and evolving specifications throughout the HC-AI SDLC.

Specifications provide a shared understanding between stakeholders, architects, engineers, AI assistants, and reviewers before engineering activities begin.

---

# 2. Objectives

The Specification Standard exists to ensure that specifications are:

- Clear
- Complete
- Consistent
- Traceable
- Verifiable
- Maintainable
- Technology-independent where appropriate
- Governed

---

# 3. Specification Principles

Specifications within the HC-AI SDLC shall follow these principles.

## 3.1 Clarity

Specifications shall communicate requirements using precise, unambiguous language.

---

## 3.2 Completeness

Specifications shall contain sufficient information to support engineering, verification, and implementation activities.

---

## 3.3 Traceability

Every specification shall remain traceable to:

- Business objectives
- Requirements
- Decisions
- Engineering artifacts
- Verification evidence

---

## 3.4 Consistency

Specifications shall use standardized terminology, structure, metadata, and formatting throughout the framework.

---

## 3.5 Maintainability

Specifications shall evolve through controlled updates while preserving version history and traceability.

---

## 3.6 Verifiability

Every requirement defined within a specification shall be capable of objective verification.

---

## 3.7 Human-Centered Design

Specifications shall prioritize human understanding before technical implementation.

---

# 4. Specification Lifecycle

Specifications shall progress through defined lifecycle stages.

Typical stages include:

- Draft
- Review
- Approved
- Active
- Revised
- Superseded
- Archived

State transitions shall be governed by the Operational Model.

---

# 5. Specification Types

The HC-AI SDLC may include specifications such as:

- Product Specifications
- Functional Specifications
- Non-Functional Specifications
- Architecture Specifications
- API Specifications
- Data Specifications
- User Experience Specifications
- Security Specifications
- Deployment Specifications
- Operational Specifications

Each specification type shall inherit this standard while defining its own specialized requirements.

---

# 6. Specification Structure

Every specification should include, where applicable:

- Purpose
- Scope
- Objectives
- Assumptions
- Constraints
- Functional Requirements
- Non-Functional Requirements
- Dependencies
- Acceptance Criteria
- Traceability References
- Version Information

Specialized specification templates may extend this structure.

---

# 7. Specification Responsibilities

Specification activities include:

- Gathering requirements
- Defining scope
- Documenting expectations
- Recording assumptions
- Identifying constraints
- Maintaining traceability
- Supporting engineering
- Supporting verification

Specifications define intent but do not prescribe implementation details unless required.

---

# 8. Specification Quality

High-quality specifications shall be:

- Accurate
- Complete
- Understandable
- Testable
- Consistent
- Feasible
- Traceable
- Maintainable

Quality shall be evaluated before engineering begins.

---

# 9. Specification Traceability

Specifications shall maintain traceability between:

- Business goals
- Requirements
- Engineering activities
- Verification activities
- Releases
- Operational records

Traceability enables governance, auditing, maintenance, and continuous improvement.

---

# 10. Specification Review

Specifications shall undergo review before approval.

Review activities may include:

- Requirement review
- Technical review
- Architecture review
- Stakeholder review
- Verification review

Review findings shall be documented.

---

# 11. Specification Evolution

Specifications may evolve as products, technologies, and business needs change.

Evolution shall:

- Preserve governance alignment.
- Maintain version history.
- Preserve traceability.
- Avoid uncontrolled requirement changes.
- Support backward compatibility where appropriate.

---

# 12. Compliance

Specifications shall comply with this standard by:

- Following approved templates.
- Maintaining required metadata.
- Remaining traceable.
- Supporting verification.
- Preserving consistency.
- Following governance requirements.

Compliance shall be verified through specification review and verification activities.

---

# Specification Declaration

The Specification Standard establishes the requirements for creating and maintaining specifications throughout the HC-AI SDLC framework.

Its purpose is to ensure that every specification provides a clear, complete, traceable, and verifiable description of intended outcomes before engineering activities begin.

This standard possesses no independent constitutional authority.

Its governing authority derives entirely from **OM-001**, which derives its authority from **AOS-001**, which derives its authority from **IAS-001**, which derives its authority from **GHS-001**, which derives its authority from **CHARTER-001**, which derives its constitutional legitimacy from the **Universal Constitution for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence (HC-001)**.

Accordingly, this standard shall always be interpreted, implemented, and maintained in complete alignment with every higher authority within the constitutional governance hierarchy.
