# RP-007 Repository Review & Compliance Standard

**Repository Family:** Repository Standards (RP)

**Identifier:** RP-007

**Version:** 1.0

**Status:** Approved

**Owner:** EOS Architecture Board

**Audience:** Engineers, Architects, Contributors, AI Assistants

---

# 1. Purpose

The Engineering Operating System (EOS) is built on the principle that engineering quality is achieved through disciplined review rather than assumption.

This standard establishes the repository review and compliance process to ensure that every engineering artifact aligns with EOS governance, engineering standards, and long-term repository integrity.

Repository reviews exist to improve engineering quality, preserve consistency, and strengthen engineering capability—not to create unnecessary administrative work.

---

# 2. Scope

This standard governs the review and compliance of all governed repository artifacts, including:

- Repository Standards (RP)
- Engineering Standards (ES)
- Engineering Profiles (EP)
- Research Standards (RS)
- Starter Kits (SK)
- Project Standards (PS)
- Architecture Decision Records (ADR)
- Templates
- Knowledge Documents
- Repository Assets where applicable

This standard applies to both newly created artifacts and modifications to existing artifacts.

---

# 3. Objectives

This standard exists to:

- Ensure repository consistency.
- Verify compliance with engineering standards.
- Improve engineering quality.
- Detect repository defects early.
- Preserve engineering governance.
- Support long-term maintainability.
- Enable continuous repository improvement.

---

# 4. Review Philosophy

Engineering quality should be verified before integration.

Every repository artifact represents engineering knowledge that future engineers will depend upon.

Repository reviews ensure that engineering knowledge is:

- correct,
- complete,
- understandable,
- consistent,
- maintainable.

Reviews strengthen engineering capability by improving knowledge rather than merely detecting mistakes.

---

# 5. Guiding Principles

## 5.1 Review Before Integration

Every governed artifact shall be reviewed before becoming an approved engineering asset.

---

## 5.2 Standards Before Preference

Repository reviews evaluate compliance with engineering standards rather than individual opinions or stylistic preferences.

---

## 5.3 Constructive Improvement

The purpose of review is continuous improvement.

Reviews should identify opportunities to strengthen engineering clarity, consistency, and quality.

---

## 5.4 Human Accountability

Artificial Intelligence may assist with repository reviews.

Final approval always remains the responsibility of human reviewers.

---

## 5.5 Transparent Decisions

Review outcomes shall be understandable, explainable, and traceable.

Engineers should understand why review decisions were made.

---

# 6. Repository Review Process

Repository reviews follow five stages.

## Stage 1 — Submission

The artifact is submitted for engineering review.

---

## Stage 2 — Automated Validation

Automated validation verifies:

- naming compliance
- classification compliance
- metadata completeness
- document structure
- reference integrity

---

## Stage 3 — Engineering Review

Human reviewers evaluate:

- engineering quality
- repository consistency
- standards compliance
- technical accuracy
- maintainability

---

## Stage 4 — Resolution

Review comments are resolved.

Required improvements are completed before approval.

---

## Stage 5 — Approval

The artifact becomes an approved engineering asset.

---

# 7. Compliance Categories

Repository compliance shall verify:

## Structural Compliance

- Repository placement
- Naming
- Classification
- Metadata

---

## Standards Compliance

- Alignment with governing standards
- Engineering consistency
- Repository governance

---

## Content Compliance

- Accuracy
- Completeness
- Clarity
- Engineering quality

---

## Governance Compliance

- Ownership
- Approval
- Versioning
- Traceability

---

# 8. Review Outcomes

Repository reviews may produce one of the following outcomes:

| Outcome | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| Approved | Meets all review requirements |
| Approved with Minor Improvements | Minor corrections required |
| Revisions Required | Significant improvements required before approval |
| Rejected | Does not satisfy repository standards |

Every outcome should include clear engineering justification.

---

# 9. Review Checklist

Repository reviewers should verify:

- correct repository family
- correct classification
- naming compliance
- metadata completeness
- engineering accuracy
- standards alignment
- repository consistency
- reference validity
- version correctness
- readability

The checklist should strengthen consistency rather than become a bureaucratic exercise.

---

# 10. AI-Assisted Review

Artificial Intelligence may assist by:

- detecting inconsistencies
- validating metadata
- identifying duplicate artifacts
- checking references
- suggesting improvements
- identifying standards violations

Artificial Intelligence shall not:

- approve repository artifacts
- override engineering judgment
- establish compliance decisions
- replace human reviewers

AI recommendations remain proposals until reviewed by engineers.

---

# 11. Responsibilities

## EOS Architecture Board

Responsible for:

- governing repository compliance
- approving review policies
- resolving review disputes
- maintaining review quality

---

## Engineers

Responsible for:

- preparing compliant artifacts
- addressing review findings
- participating in engineering reviews
- maintaining repository quality

---

## AI Assistants

**Artificial Intelligence operates within the engineering boundaries established by approved human governance. AI may assist, analyze, recommend, and validate. Final engineering authority always remains with human engineers.**



- performing automated validation
- identifying potential issues
- improving review efficiency

AI operates within human-defined engineering standards.

---

# 12. Compliance Requirements

Every governed artifact shall satisfy:

- RP-001 Foundation
- RP-002 Repository Taxonomy
- RP-003 Repository Naming Convention
- RP-004 Repository Classification Standard
- RP-005 Repository Evolution & Migration Standard
- RP-006 Repository Metadata Standard

Repository artifacts that fail compliance shall not be approved until deficiencies are resolved.

---

# 13. Review Records

Repository reviews should maintain sufficient records to support engineering traceability.

Review records should include:

- artifact reviewed
- reviewer
- review outcome
- significant findings
- approval date
- version reviewed

Review documentation should support future engineering understanding without creating unnecessary administrative overhead.

---

# 14. Governance

Repository review processes are governed by the EOS Architecture Board.

Changes to review procedures require Architecture Board approval.

Repository governance shall prioritize engineering quality, consistency, and transparency over review speed.

---

# 15. Maintenance

Review procedures should evolve only when they produce measurable improvements in engineering quality or repository governance.

Automation should reduce repetitive review effort while preserving human judgment.

Continuous improvement should strengthen engineering capability rather than increase process complexity.

---

# 16. Constitutional Statement

Repository review is the engineering discipline of validating knowledge before it becomes part of the Engineering Operating System.

Reviews exist to strengthen engineering quality, preserve repository integrity, and ensure that future engineers can trust the knowledge they inherit.

Artificial Intelligence may accelerate validation, identify inconsistencies, and improve review efficiency.

Humans remain responsible for engineering judgment, approval, accountability, and the long-term quality of the Engineering Operating System.
