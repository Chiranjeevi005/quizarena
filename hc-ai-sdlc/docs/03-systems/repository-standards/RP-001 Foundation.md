# RP-001 Foundation

**Repository Family:** Repository Standards (RP)
**Identifier:** RP-001
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active
**Owner:** EOS Team
**Audience:** All Engineering Personnel, Platform Architects, and Automated Systems

## Purpose
This document serves as the constitutional foundation for the Engineering Operating System (EOS) repository. It establishes the baseline philosophy, human-centric governance model, and core principles that all future repository architecture, structural standards, and automated interactions MUST inherit.

## Scope
This standard governs the overarching design, management, and evolution of the EOS repository itself. It applies to all directories, files, naming conventions, structural changes, and automated tooling operating within the repository.

## Objectives
- Establish the repository as a rigorously engineered, long-term enterprise platform.
- Codify the irrefutable authority of human engineers over the repository's structure and contents.
- Define the boundaries and constraints of artificial intelligence and automated systems interacting with the repository.
- Provide a timeless, immutable baseline from which all other Repository Standards (RP) inherit.

## Definitions
- **Engineering Operating System (EOS):** The comprehensive platform encompassing the standards, profiles, templates, and architecture required to execute engineering workloads.
- **Repository Standard (RP):** A governed document dictating the architectural, structural, or administrative rules of the EOS repository.
- **Human Authority:** The principle that all strategic decisions, standard definitions, and accountability rest exclusively with human engineering personnel.

## Guiding Principles
1. **The Repository is a Product:** The repository structure is a first-class engineering artifact that demands the same rigor, design, and maintenance as production software.
2. **Cognitive Simplicity:** Every structural decision MUST prioritize minimizing the cognitive load required for an engineer to navigate, understand, and contribute.
3. **Enduring Stability:** The foundation of the repository MUST be designed to last for a decade, accommodating scaling without requiring fundamental restructuring.

## Normative Rules
1. The repository MUST adhere to a strict, lifecycle-based structural taxonomy.
2. All Repository Standards (RP) MUST inherit from and remain subordinate to this foundational document (RP-001).
3. No automated system or artificial intelligence MAY unilaterally alter the root architecture, taxonomy, or governance models of the repository.
4. Structural additions or modifications MUST NOT violate the principle of single responsibility (one folder, one obvious purpose).

## Responsibilities
- **EOS Architects (Humans):** MUST define the repository's purpose, establish structural standards, make all architectural decisions, and accept ultimate responsibility for the repository's health.
- **AI Assistants:** **Artificial Intelligence operates within the engineering boundaries established by approved human governance. AI may assist, analyze, recommend, and validate. Final engineering authority always remains with human engineers.**

## Relationships
- **Inherits:** None (Constitutional Document)
- **Precedes:** All other Repository Standards (e.g., RP-002, RP-003)
- **Constrains:** The design and evolution of the entire EOS repository ecosystem.

## Compliance Requirements
- All structural changes to the repository MUST be reviewed and explicitly approved by a designated human architect.
- Any repository standard or automation tool found to conflict with the human-centric authority defined herein MUST be immediately disabled and remediated.

## Exceptions
- There are no exceptions to the human authority requirement.
- Temporary structural deviations during major migrations MAY be permitted if explicitly authorized by the EOS Lead Architect and documented in an Architecture Decision Record (ADR).

## Governance
This constitutional standard is strictly governed by the highest engineering authority (the EOS Architecture Board). Modification of this document requires consensus approval and exhaustive review, as it cascades to all other repository standards.

## Maintenance
This document is considered timeless and SHOULD NOT require frequent modification. Reviews MUST be conducted every two years to ensure technological shifts have not invalidated its foundational principles.

## Success Criteria
- The repository scales seamlessly over multiple years without requiring a major structural overhaul.
- Automated systems assist engineers flawlessly without executing unapproved structural modifications.
- New engineers can navigate and contribute to the repository with zero structural ambiguity.

## Future Evolution
Changes to this foundation are inherently high-risk and MUST only occur when a paradigm-shifting change in repository management (e.g., transitioning away from Git, or fundamentally redefining the engineering lifecycle) necessitates rewriting the EOS constitution.
