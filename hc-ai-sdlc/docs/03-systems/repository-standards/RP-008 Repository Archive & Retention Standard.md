# RP-008 Repository Archive & Retention Standard

**Repository Family:** Repository Standards (RP)

**Identifier:** RP-008

**Version:** 1.0

**Status:** Approved

**Owner:** EOS Architecture Board

**Audience:** Engineers, Architects, Contributors, AI Assistants

---

# 1. Purpose

The Engineering Operating System (EOS) is designed to preserve engineering knowledge throughout its lifecycle.

Not all engineering artifacts remain active indefinitely.

This standard establishes the governing principles for repository archiving and retention to ensure that obsolete, superseded, or inactive artifacts are managed without compromising repository clarity, engineering traceability, or historical knowledge.

Archiving exists to preserve engineering history while maintaining an efficient and understandable active repository.

---

# 2. Scope

This standard governs the archival and retention of all repository artifacts, including:

- Repository Standards (RP)
- Engineering Standards (ES)
- Engineering Profiles (EP)
- Research Standards (RS)
- Starter Kits (SK)
- Project Standards (PS)
- Architecture Decision Records (ADR)
- Templates
- Knowledge Documents
- Reference Documents
- Repository Assets

This standard applies to every governed engineering artifact within EOS.

---

# 3. Objectives

This standard exists to:

- Preserve engineering history.
- Maintain repository clarity.
- Prevent accidental knowledge loss.
- Improve repository maintainability.
- Support engineering traceability.
- Enable long-term repository governance.
- Reduce active repository complexity.

---

# 4. Archive Philosophy

Engineering knowledge should not be deleted simply because it is no longer active.

Historical engineering decisions provide valuable context for future engineers.

The repository should preserve engineering history while ensuring that only current artifacts guide active engineering work.

Archiving separates historical knowledge from active engineering without destroying either.

---

# 5. Guiding Principles

## 5.1 Preserve Knowledge

Engineering knowledge should be preserved whenever it provides historical, educational, legal, architectural, or governance value.

---

## 5.2 Active Repository First

Only actively governed artifacts should remain within the active repository structure.

Inactive artifacts should be archived rather than left alongside current engineering guidance.

---

## 5.3 Traceability

Every archived artifact should remain traceable to its historical purpose.

Engineers should understand:

- why it existed,
- why it was archived,
- what replaced it (if applicable).

---

## 5.4 Controlled Removal

Deletion is exceptional.

Archiving is the preferred repository strategy.

Repository deletion should occur only when approved by repository governance.

---

## 5.5 Human Governance

Repository archival decisions remain human responsibilities.

Artificial Intelligence may recommend archival opportunities but shall not archive or delete repository artifacts autonomously.

---

# 6. Archive Eligibility

Repository artifacts may be archived when they are:

- superseded
- deprecated
- obsolete
- no longer actively maintained
- replaced by newer standards
- completed historical deliverables
- inactive reference material

Artifacts shall not be archived solely because they are old.

Age alone is not a valid archival criterion.

---

# 7. Archive Categories

Repository archives may include:

## Superseded

Artifacts replaced by newer governed versions.

---

## Deprecated

Artifacts no longer recommended for future engineering work.

---

## Historical

Artifacts retained for engineering history.

---

## Reference

Artifacts preserved for educational or contextual purposes.

---

## Retired

Artifacts whose engineering responsibility has permanently ended.

---

# 8. Retention Principles

Archived artifacts shall:

- remain readable,
- preserve engineering integrity,
- retain original identifiers,
- preserve version history,
- remain discoverable.

Archived artifacts shall not be modified except when required for governance or repository maintenance.

---

# 9. Archive Process

Repository archival follows five controlled stages.

## Stage 1 — Assessment

Determine whether archival criteria have been satisfied.

---

## Stage 2 — Validation

Confirm that the artifact is no longer the active engineering authority.

---

## Stage 3 — Approval

Obtain Architecture Board approval when required.

---

## Stage 4 — Archive

Move the artifact to the approved archive location.

Preserve:

- identifier
- version
- metadata
- traceability

---

## Stage 5 — Verification

Verify:

- archive integrity
- repository references
- discoverability
- historical traceability

---

# 10. Retention Rules

Archived artifacts shall:

- preserve original filenames,
- preserve metadata,
- preserve engineering relationships,
- preserve repository references where practical.

Repository history should remain understandable to future engineers.

---

# 11. Deletion Policy

Repository deletion is strongly discouraged.

Deletion may occur only when:

- duplicate artifacts have no engineering value,
- repository corruption requires recovery,
- legal or regulatory obligations require removal,
- Architecture Board approval has been granted.

Deletion shall never compromise repository integrity or engineering traceability.

---

# 12. Responsibilities

## EOS Architecture Board

Responsible for:

- approving archival policies,
- governing repository retention,
- approving permanent deletion,
- maintaining archive integrity.

---

## Engineers

Responsible for:

- identifying archival candidates,
- maintaining repository accuracy,
- preserving engineering traceability.

---

## AI Assistants

**Artificial Intelligence operates within the engineering boundaries established by approved human governance. AI may assist, analyze, recommend, and validate. Final engineering authority always remains with human engineers.**



- identify inactive artifacts,
- detect superseded documents,
- recommend archival opportunities,
- validate archive consistency.

Artificial Intelligence shall not archive or delete repository artifacts without explicit human approval.

---

# 13. Compliance

Repository archival shall comply with:

- RP-001 Foundation
- RP-002 Repository Taxonomy
- RP-003 Repository Naming Convention
- RP-004 Repository Classification Standard
- RP-005 Repository Evolution & Migration Standard
- RP-006 Repository Metadata Standard
- RP-007 Repository Review & Compliance Standard

Repository archival shall preserve repository integrity and engineering governance.

---

# 14. Governance

Repository archive policies are governed by the EOS Architecture Board.

Changes to archival principles require Architecture Board approval.

Repository history is considered an engineering asset and shall be protected accordingly.

---

# 15. Maintenance

Archive policies should evolve only when they improve repository governance, engineering maintainability, or long-term knowledge preservation.

Repository growth should be managed through disciplined archival rather than uncontrolled accumulation.

Engineering history should remain accessible without increasing active repository complexity.

---

# 16. Constitutional Statement

The Engineering Operating System preserves engineering knowledge as a long-term organizational asset.

Archiving is not the removal of engineering knowledge.

It is the disciplined preservation of engineering history while maintaining clarity within the active repository.

Artificial Intelligence may assist in identifying archival opportunities and validating repository consistency.

Humans remain responsible for deciding what should be preserved, what should remain active, and what may be permanently removed.

EOS protects both the future of engineering and the history that made it possible.
