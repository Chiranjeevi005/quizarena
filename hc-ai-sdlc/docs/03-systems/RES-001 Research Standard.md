# RES-001 — Research Standard

> **Normative System Standard**
>
> This standard establishes the principles, responsibilities, methods, and quality expectations for research conducted within the Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence Software Development Lifecycle (HC-AI SDLC).
>
> Research provides the evidence required to support informed decisions before, during, and after engineering activities.
>
> This standard derives its governing authority from **OM-001** and ultimately from the **Universal Constitution for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence (HC-001)**.

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this standard is to establish a consistent research methodology throughout the HC-AI SDLC.

Research shall reduce uncertainty, improve decision quality, validate assumptions, and support evidence-based engineering.

---

# 2. Objectives

The Research Standard exists to ensure research activities are:

- Objective
- Evidence-based
- Traceable
- Repeatable
- Ethical
- Governed
- Actionable
- Maintainable

---

# 3. Research Principles

Research within the HC-AI SDLC shall follow these principles.

## 3.1 Evidence First

Engineering decisions shall be supported by objective evidence whenever reasonably possible.

---

## 3.2 Human-Centered

Research shall prioritize human needs, organizational goals, and stakeholder value.

---

## 3.3 Objectivity

Research shall distinguish between observations, evidence, assumptions, interpretations, and recommendations.

---

## 3.4 Traceability

Research findings shall remain traceable to:

- Sources
- Requirements
- Decisions
- Engineering artifacts
- Operational outcomes

---

## 3.5 Reproducibility

Research methods shall be documented sufficiently to enable independent review or repetition where appropriate.

---

## 3.6 Transparency

Research limitations, uncertainties, and assumptions shall be explicitly documented.

---

## 3.7 Continuous Learning

Research shall contribute to continuous improvement of products, engineering practices, and the HC-AI SDLC framework.

---

# 4. Research Lifecycle

Research activities typically progress through the following stages:

- Problem Identification
- Objective Definition
- Research Planning
- Evidence Collection
- Analysis
- Synthesis
- Recommendation
- Review
- Documentation

Each stage shall support informed engineering decisions.

---

# 5. Research Types

Research within the HC-AI SDLC may include:

- Business Research
- User Research
- Product Research
- Technical Research
- Market Research
- Architecture Research
- Technology Evaluation
- Risk Assessment
- Feasibility Study
- Performance Analysis

Each research type shall inherit this standard while defining its own specialized practices.

---

# 6. Research Activities

Research activities include:

- Identifying research questions
- Collecting evidence
- Evaluating sources
- Comparing alternatives
- Identifying risks
- Validating assumptions
- Documenting findings
- Supporting engineering decisions

---

# 7. Research Evidence

Research evidence may include:

- Documents
- Interviews
- Surveys
- Observations
- Experimental results
- Benchmarks
- Metrics
- Industry standards
- Academic publications
- Internal records

Evidence quality shall be evaluated before use.

---

# 8. Research Quality

Research quality shall emphasize:

- Accuracy
- Relevance
- Completeness
- Credibility
- Objectivity
- Timeliness
- Repeatability
- Traceability

Research findings shall clearly distinguish verified evidence from opinion.

---

# 9. Research Documentation

Research outputs shall include, where applicable:

- Objectives
- Methodology
- Sources
- Findings
- Analysis
- Assumptions
- Risks
- Recommendations
- References

Documentation shall support future review and knowledge reuse.

---

# 10. Research Traceability

Research shall maintain traceability between:

- Research questions
- Evidence
- Findings
- Engineering decisions
- Specifications
- Verification
- Products

Traceability supports governance, accountability, and continuous learning.

---

# 11. Research Evolution

Research practices may evolve as methodologies, technologies, and organizational needs change.

Evolution shall:

- Preserve governance alignment.
- Improve research quality.
- Encourage evidence-based engineering.
- Maintain methodological consistency.

---

# 12. Compliance

Research activities shall comply with this standard by:

- Following documented methodologies.
- Using credible evidence.
- Maintaining traceability.
- Documenting assumptions.
- Supporting engineering decisions.
- Preserving research integrity.

Compliance shall be verified through research review and governance assessment.

---

# Research Declaration

The Research Standard establishes the expectations for research conducted throughout the HC-AI SDLC framework.

Its purpose is to ensure that engineering and operational decisions are supported by objective, traceable, and well-documented evidence while remaining fully aligned with governance and operational standards.

This standard possesses no independent constitutional authority.

Its governing authority derives entirely from **OM-001**, which derives its authority from **AOS-001**, which derives its authority from **IAS-001**, which derives its authority from **GHS-001**, which derives its authority from **CHARTER-001**, which derives its constitutional legitimacy from the **Universal Constitution for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence (HC-001)**.

Accordingly, this standard shall always be interpreted, implemented, and maintained in complete alignment with every higher authority within the constitutional governance hierarchy.
