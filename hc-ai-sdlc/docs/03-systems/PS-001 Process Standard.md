# PS-001 — Process Standard

> **Normative System Standard**
>
> This standard establishes the principles, structure, governance, and quality expectations for operational processes within the Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence Software Development Lifecycle (HC-AI SDLC).
>
> Processes define how work is consistently executed, coordinated, monitored, improved, and governed throughout the lifecycle.
>
> This standard derives its governing authority from **OM-001** and ultimately from the **Universal Constitution for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence (HC-001)**.

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this standard is to establish a consistent operational process framework throughout the HC-AI SDLC.

Processes provide repeatable methods for transforming governed requirements into reliable engineering outcomes while maintaining quality, traceability, and continuous improvement.

---

# 2. Objectives

The Process Standard exists to ensure operational processes are:

- Repeatable
- Governed
- Consistent
- Traceable
- Efficient
- Measurable
- Adaptable
- Continuously improved

---

# 3. Process Principles

Operational processes within the HC-AI SDLC shall follow these principles.

## 3.1 Governance Alignment

Every process shall comply with applicable governance, operational, and engineering standards.

---

## 3.2 Defined Responsibility

Every process shall have clearly defined ownership, responsibilities, inputs, outputs, and expected outcomes.

---

## 3.3 Repeatability

Processes shall be documented sufficiently to enable consistent execution across teams and projects.

---

## 3.4 Traceability

Process execution shall remain traceable from initiation through completion.

---

## 3.5 Continuous Improvement

Processes shall evolve through observation, measurement, verification, and lessons learned.

---

## 3.6 Simplicity

Processes shall minimize unnecessary complexity while maintaining governance and engineering quality.

---

## 3.7 Human-Centered Execution

Processes shall support effective collaboration between people and AI assistants while preserving human accountability.

---

# 4. Process Lifecycle

Operational processes typically progress through the following stages:

- Initiation
- Planning
- Preparation
- Execution
- Monitoring
- Review
- Completion
- Improvement

Each process shall define its own entry and exit criteria.

---

# 5. Process Types

The HC-AI SDLC may include processes such as:

- Planning Process
- Requirements Process
- Design Process
- Engineering Process
- Verification Process
- Review Process
- Change Management Process
- Release Process
- Maintenance Process
- Incident Management Process
- Continuous Improvement Process

Each specialized process shall inherit this standard.

---

# 6. Process Components

Every operational process should define:

- Purpose
- Scope
- Roles
- Inputs
- Activities
- Outputs
- Decision Points
- Dependencies
- Success Criteria
- Completion Criteria

---

# 7. Process Responsibilities

Operational processes shall clearly define responsibilities for:

- Initiating work
- Performing activities
- Reviewing outputs
- Making decisions
- Recording evidence
- Managing risks
- Closing activities
- Supporting continuous improvement

Responsibilities shall remain explicit throughout process execution.

---

# 8. Process Quality

Operational processes shall emphasize:

- Consistency
- Reliability
- Efficiency
- Transparency
- Quality
- Traceability
- Maintainability

Quality shall be continuously monitored and improved.

---

# 9. Process Measurement

Processes should be evaluated using measurable indicators.

Examples include:

- Completion rate
- Cycle time
- Review effectiveness
- Defect rate
- Rework rate
- Process compliance
- Automation coverage
- Improvement outcomes

Measurements shall support operational improvement rather than individual evaluation.

---

# 10. Process Documentation

Each operational process shall be documented sufficiently to support:

- Execution
- Training
- Verification
- Automation
- Continuous improvement
- Knowledge transfer

Documentation shall remain synchronized with operational changes.

---

# 11. Process Evolution

Operational processes may evolve as engineering practices, organizational needs, and technologies change.

Evolution shall:

- Preserve governance alignment.
- Maintain operational consistency.
- Improve effectiveness.
- Reduce unnecessary complexity.
- Preserve traceability.

---

# 12. Compliance

Operational processes shall comply with this standard by:

- Following approved process definitions.
- Maintaining traceability.
- Recording execution evidence.
- Supporting verification.
- Measuring effectiveness.
- Continuously improving execution.

Compliance shall be verified through process reviews and operational assessments.

---

# Process Declaration

The Process Standard establishes the operational process expectations for the HC-AI SDLC framework.

Its purpose is to ensure that engineering and operational activities are executed through consistent, governed, traceable, and continuously improving processes.

This standard possesses no independent constitutional authority.

Its governing authority derives entirely from **OM-001**, which derives its authority from **AOS-001**, which derives its authority from **IAS-001**, which derives its authority from **GHS-001**, which derives its authority from **CHARTER-001**, which derives its constitutional legitimacy from the **Universal Constitution for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence (HC-001)**.

Accordingly, this standard shall always be interpreted, implemented, and maintained in complete alignment with every higher authority within the constitutional governance hierarchy.
