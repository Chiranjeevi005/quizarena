# EP-001 — Engineering Profile Standard

> **Normative System Standard**
>
> This standard establishes the principles, structure, governance, and lifecycle of Engineering Profiles within the Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence Software Development Lifecycle (HC-AI SDLC).
>
> Engineering Profiles define standardized responsibilities, capabilities, expected outputs, and governance for engineering roles performed by humans, AI assistants, or hybrid human–AI collaboration.
>
> This standard derives its governing authority from **OM-001** and ultimately from the **Universal Constitution for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence (HC-001)**.

---

# 1. Purpose

The purpose of this standard is to establish a consistent framework for defining engineering roles and responsibilities across the HC-AI SDLC.

Engineering Profiles provide reusable operational definitions that improve consistency, collaboration, accountability, and engineering quality.

---

# 2. Objectives

The Engineering Profile Standard exists to ensure engineering profiles are:

- Clearly defined
- Reusable
- Consistent
- Traceable
- Governed
- Role-oriented
- Technology-independent
- Maintainable

---

# 3. Engineering Profile Principles

Engineering Profiles shall follow these principles.

## 3.1 Role Clarity

Every Engineering Profile shall define a clearly identifiable engineering responsibility.

---

## 3.2 Separation of Responsibilities

Profiles shall describe responsibilities without overlapping unrelated engineering domains.

---

## 3.3 Reusability

Profiles shall be reusable across products, projects, teams, and organizations.

---

## 3.4 Human-Centered Collaboration

Profiles shall support work performed by:

- Human engineers
- AI assistants
- Human-AI collaborative teams

while preserving human accountability.

---

## 3.5 Traceability

Engineering responsibilities shall remain traceable to operational activities, engineering outputs, verification activities, and governance requirements.

---

## 3.6 Consistency

All Engineering Profiles shall follow a common structure, terminology, and metadata.

---

## 3.7 Continuous Improvement

Engineering Profiles shall evolve as engineering practices and technologies mature.

---

# 4. Engineering Profile Components

Every Engineering Profile should define:

- Purpose
- Scope
- Responsibilities
- Required competencies
- Expected inputs
- Expected outputs
- Dependencies
- Collaboration requirements
- Success criteria
- Operational boundaries

---

# 5. Engineering Profile Types

Engineering Profiles may include:

- Business Analyst
- Product Manager
- Solution Architect
- Software Architect
- Backend Engineer
- Frontend Engineer
- Full Stack Engineer
- AI Engineer
- DevOps Engineer
- QA Engineer
- Security Engineer
- Data Engineer
- UX Designer
- Technical Writer
- Project Manager

Additional profiles may be introduced as organizational needs evolve.

---

# 6. Engineering Responsibilities

Engineering Profiles define responsibilities such as:

- Analysis
- Planning
- Design
- Engineering
- Verification
- Documentation
- Collaboration
- Continuous Improvement

Profiles describe responsibilities rather than prescribing implementation methods.

---

# 7. Engineering Collaboration

Engineering Profiles shall support effective collaboration between:

- Engineering disciplines
- Operational roles
- Governance functions
- AI assistants
- External stakeholders

Collaboration responsibilities shall be explicitly defined where applicable.

---

# 8. Engineering Quality

Engineering Profiles shall promote:

- Professional accountability
- Engineering excellence
- Knowledge sharing
- Consistency
- Quality
- Continuous learning

Quality expectations apply regardless of implementation technology.

---

# 9. Engineering Lifecycle

Engineering Profiles remain active throughout the engineering lifecycle.

Responsibilities may change depending upon:

- Operational stage
- Engineering activity
- Product maturity
- Organizational needs

Lifecycle changes shall preserve governance alignment.

---

# 10. Engineering Traceability

Engineering Profiles shall maintain traceability between:

- Operational responsibilities
- Engineering activities
- Specifications
- Verification activities
- Deliverables
- Governance requirements

Traceability supports accountability and continuous improvement.

---

# 11. Engineering Evolution

Engineering Profiles may evolve to reflect:

- Emerging technologies
- Organizational growth
- Industry practices
- AI capabilities
- Engineering maturity

Evolution shall preserve consistency and governance.

---

# 12. Compliance

Engineering Profiles shall comply with this standard by:

- Following the approved profile structure.
- Clearly defining responsibilities.
- Maintaining governance alignment.
- Supporting operational execution.
- Maintaining traceability.
- Supporting continuous improvement.

Compliance shall be verified through engineering review and governance assessment.

---

# Engineering Profile Declaration

The Engineering Profile Standard establishes the framework for defining reusable engineering roles throughout the HC-AI SDLC.

Its purpose is to ensure engineering responsibilities remain clear, governed, traceable, reusable, and consistently applied across products, projects, and organizations.

This standard possesses no independent constitutional authority.

Its governing authority derives entirely from **OM-001**, which derives its authority from **AOS-001**, which derives its authority from **IAS-001**, which derives its authority from **GHS-001**, which derives its authority from **CHARTER-001**, which derives its constitutional legitimacy from the **Universal Constitution for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence (HC-001)**.

Accordingly, this standard shall always be interpreted, implemented, and maintained in complete alignment with every higher authority within the constitutional governance hierarchy.
